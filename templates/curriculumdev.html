Openings

Techtonica is a nonprofit that offers six months of full-time software engineering training with living stipends to Bay Area women and non-binary adults with low incomes. After graduation, Techtonica places graduates with engineering jobs at local companies. You can read more here.

Make an Impact as Our Curriculum Developer!

Techtonica offers a life-changing learning experience to participants. We care deeply about helping our apprentices become high-quality developers.

We're looking for a curriculum developer to develop a software engineering curriculum for our six month coding bootcamp. We have an existing curriculum from our first 2018 cohort that you will build upon and improve. This is a part-time position.

Responsibilities:

  • Develop the overall plan and pacing for the curriculum, including the balance of lessons, projects, and assessments, while incorporating feedback from the first cohort of apprentices
  • Develop lesson plans, projects, and assessments (building upon existing materials)
  • Develop pre-work to for apprentices to complete before in-person bootcamp starts
  • Break down necessary work into individual pieces that can be developed by volunteers
  • Review work of volunteers for accuracy, completeness, and overall quality

What we're looking for:

  • You can clearly explain technical topics in writing
  • You understand what is needed to become an entry-level software engineer
  • You understand how to explain technical topics in a way that is rigorous but can be understood by beginners
  • You have an understanding of socioeconomic difference and experiences that our students face

Preferred previous experience:

  • Previous experience developing a technical curriculum
  • Previous teaching experience preferred
  • Previous experience as a professional software developer preferred
