Apprenticeship

Techtonica's apprenticeship is intensive, hands-on tech learning for local women and non-binary adults with low incomes.

The Program

Apprentices will learn to do full-stack web development using JavaScript. What bootcamps and apprenticeships like Techtonica have in common is the fact that they are intensive training. However, bootcamps can be pretty passive classroom learning with students and they cost between $15,000 and $30,000. An apprenticeship aims to be more like the workplace to make the transition into the industry better—instead of having an instructor constantly guiding you, a facilitator gives you assignments and you're expected to figure out what to do as a team. In the best-case scenarios, there are regular check-ins with managers and mentors provide support. We’re making Techtonica’s program as hands-on and project-based as possible to best prepare you for work in tech.

After the first trial month, participants who work the hardest and meet and exceed expectations will be invited to continue for five months with stipends. Upon successful completion of six months, as many apprentices as possible are placed with partner companies via a two-way interview and rating system.


The next apprenticeship starts in July 2018 in San Francisco.

Eligibility

You are eligible to apply for Techtonica’s apprenticeship if you:

  • are a woman or non-binary, feminine-adjacent adult
  • are digitally literate, meaning you are very familiar and good with computers and the internet, can type 40+ WPM, and you know how to use a mouse/trackpad, browsers, and email
  • meet our socioeconomic criteria (less than $50,000 in income and net worth)
  • have housing that will be stable for the duration of the program
  • have lived in the Bay Area for at least a year
  • can legally be employed by our partner companies
  • are prepared to appear in Techtonica's media and pitch yourself to Techtonica's partners
  • have not attended a full-time coding bootcamp or completed a C.S. degree in the last six years

Application Process

1. Fill out the interest form.

Fill out the interest form completely. Submit as soon as you can and watch for a follow-up email.

2. If invited, join one of our introductions.

Learn how a web page is made and code your own while being assessed. There will be several sessions to choose from in Oakland and San Francisco.

3. Attend a coding workshop.

There will be workshops for you to choose from in Oakland and San Francisco.

4. Complete the take-home challenge.

We'll give you a coding challenge to work on at home.

5. Fill out the application.

Thoughtfully complete the long application.

6. Interview with a staff member and a board member.

Schedule a time to meet us individually to be assessed for fit.

What We Look For

This program is very competitive; we select participants who continuously prove that they are:

  • self-motivated
  • resilient
  • logical
  • humble
  • respectful
  • persistent
  • optimistic
  • punctual
  • committed
  • collaborative
  • responsive to feedback
  • willing to ask questions
  • long-term goal setters
  • able to deal with stress well
  • able and willing to work with anyone
  • take pride in owning their learning
  • always curious and ready to learn and improve
  • transparent and compassionate communicators
  • hard-working and ready to make sacrifices
  • dedicated to seeing things through to the end
  • willing to meet the apprentice expectations, including abiding by Techtonica's Code of Conduct

How to Prepare

1. Learn as much JavaScript ahead of time as possible—Free Code Camp and Codecademy are great for this.

2. Attend coding meetups, like the Women Who Code JavaScript study group.

3. Develop your typing and Mac keyboard shortcut skills.

4. Make sure your life is ready to take care of itself for a while (automate your bill-paying and make freezer meals to save time, for example).

5. Use social media to start building a network of people who can help you when you get stuck.

6. Make a list of things that re-charge you so you can turn to it when needed.
