Join our team as our instructor!

Techtonica is a new nonprofit organization. We offer low-income women and non-binary adults free tech training, along with living and childcare stipends, and job placement.

Make an Impact

Techtonica offers an unforgettable learning experience to students. We care deeply about helping students become high-quality developers. We are looking for a committed instructor to complement our education program. You will be responsible for preparing and implementing a teaching plan according to the course's requirments. It will be fundamental to provide knowledge and instruction to students in addition to helping them develop their personal and professional skills. The ideal candidate will be passionate for the job with an ability to reach out to students and create a relationship of mutual trust. They will know how to organize a class and make learning an easy and meaningful process. The goal is to help cultivate the students' interest in tech and be their dedicated ally in the process of learning and development.

Responsibilities:

  • Present lessons in a comprehensive manner and use visual/audio means to facilitate learning
  • Develop and enrich professional skills and knowledge by attending seminars, conferences, etc.
  • Create and distribute educational content (notes, summaries, assignments, etc.)
  • Provide individualized instruction to each student by promoting interactive learning
  • Monday-Friday 9am-6pm, ~1 weekday evening, 1-2 Saturdays per month for six months

What we're looking for:

  • Someone who can familiarize themselves with the cohort and Techtonica's vision quickly
  • Excellent communicability and interpersonal skills
  • Strong moral values and discipline
  • Proven experience as a teacher
  • Well-organized and committed
  • Being aware and respectful of introvert's needs but with an extrovert personality.
  • A minimum of two years of professional experience in full-stack web development
  • Forthcoming to different situations, putting personal biases aside
  • Understanding of socioeconomic difference and experiences that our students face

This role includes:

  • Working closely with the founder on the program vision
  • Organizing one-day workshops with local organizations and building community partnerships
  • Collaborating on designing and implementing project-based curriculum with sponsoring companies
  • Managing volunteers and mentors during workshops and volunteering that concerns the curriculum, students, etc.
  • Teaching with a focus on empowering students so they can succeed long-term
  • Being aware and respectful of introvert's needs but with an extrovert personality.

Because this role involves a lot of people-interacting, it might be best if you're extroverted or at least an ambivert, but you should also respect the space needed by introverts, especially since you'll be working closely with the founder, who is an introvert.

See some of the subjects we aim to cover here.

The interview process involves an interview (online or on-site), writing a lesson plan, and teaching a technical concept. If given a second interview, it will involve meeting with the first cohort. Please send a link to your LinkedIn profile, other personal page, or resume.
