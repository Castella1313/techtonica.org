Techtonica

Free tech training and job placement for low-income women and genderqueer adults

What We Do

We provide tuition-free tech training to local, low-income women and genderqueer adults and place them in positions at companies that are ready to support more diverse teams.

Why

Though many jobs are available in top tech market cities like San Francisco, companies need to build teams with more racial, gender, and class diversity. Huge income disparities also exist in these areas.

Other technical training programs cost upwards of $15K, require laptops, do little to support parents, and provide limited transitions into the workforce.

Trial Workshops

Techtonica works with local organizations to conduct free, one-day workshops.

Vetting

We filter for interest, understanding, team-playing, resilience, commitment, and responsibility by asking students to teach before inviting them to apply.

Partner Companies

Partner companies fund the training through pre-paid recruitment fees. Students are aligned with mentors and with a company that will employ them.

Training

Accepted students receive laptops, a living costs stipend, and childcare. They receive six months of full-time training that includes lectures, building assignments, projects, and experienced feedback.
